# Self CheckOut at Safeway 

I went for a quick run to Safeway for groceries right after school. Before entering, I put my backpack and bike helmet in my cart, then made my way inside Safeway. After scanning some of my items for deals to be applied when I check out, I chose the self-checkout line since I only had a few items.

<img width="200" height="300" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-01 at 11 39 43 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2259379f-db43-45a1-a9f5-846042ce322d" />

When I first got to the machine, it prompted me to scan a membership card if I had one. I was confused because I didn't have a physical card, and without it my scanned deals wouldn't transfer from my app. My **mental model** was that I could scan my phone on the machine for it to read my account. With help from a worker, I learned I had to enter my phone number manually, and I was then able to complete this step.

The process was easier after that. While scanning my items, I became comfortable because of the immediate audio feedback, with a short beep after scanning each item. This is a good example of **feedback**, as I was assured after completing a task that I was doing it right, especially because of the familiarity I’ve seen with how cashiers scan items.

<img width="219" height="251" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-01 at 11 48 59 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fb3409d3-0890-453f-a335-7877c315c281" />

However, this was not the end of experience. When I tried to push pay an unexpected popup appeared. 

<img width="223" height="172" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-01 at 11 47 41 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2d9d91da-fd19-4f5f-94a0-58cc2c0c1b13" />

The system displayed a big message on the screen saying that there were still items in my cart that needed to be scanned. It even highlighted the items in my cart. I was confused about what could possibly be in there. Then I realized that the system was flagging my backpack and helmet. This is an example of **error prevention**, as the system tries to stop users from making mistakes, like if they had forgotten to scan items in the cart. Yet in my case, it created a problem. The system was trying to help but instead blocked me from completing the checkout. Thankfully, a worker saw my issue and was able to clear it so I could complete the checkout.
