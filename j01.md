# Self CheckOut at Safeway 

I went for a Safeway run for groceries right after school. Before entering I put my backpack and helmet in my cart, I then made my way through doors inside Safeway. Afteringm scanning some of my items for deals on the app so that they can be applied when I check out I choose the self-checkout line as I had few items.

When I got to first got to the machine, the prompt spoke to me and said to scan a membership card if I had one. I was confused at first because I didn't have one and wihtout it the deals woudl be able to transfer from my app. The **mental model** was throw off. I thought I would scan my app on the machine for it to read my account. After a worker helped me figure that out that all I need ot do was input my number I got that done.

Then it was after I started scanning my items that I become comfortable because it felt familiar this is a good exmaple of **feedback**. It was because of the beep i had after I scacnned each item that I knew I was doing it right. Especially because that's how I’ve seen cashiers scan items

This was not the end of it though. when i tried to push  I was allowed to pu to he machine suddenly stopped me and displayed a message saying there were still items in my cart that needed to be scanned. This was confusing because I had already scanned everything I planned to buy. It turned out the system detected my backpack and helmet as unscanned items. This is an example of error prevention, where the system tries to stop users from making mistakes, but in this case it was too sensitive and created a problem instead.

The message itself wasn’t very clear, which made the situation more frustrating. Even though the system was trying to help, the lack of clear explanation made it harder to understand what was wrong. I had to wait for a worker to come over and confirm that those items were not part of my purchase. After pressing a few buttons, I was finally able to continue and complete the checkout.

Overall, parts of the experience worked well, especially the scanning process, which felt intuitive and easy to follow. However, the interruption caused by the system misidentifying items in my cart slowed everything down and made the self-checkout less convenient than expected.
