# Self CheckOut at Safeway 

I went for a Safeway run for groceries right after school. Before entering I put my backpack and helmet in my cart, I then made my way through doors inside Safeway. Afteringm scanning some of my items for deals on the app so that they can be applied when I check out I choose the self-checkout line as I had few items.

![111](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/72b18a86-814c-4df2-9fee-67304880746a)

When I got to first got to the machine, it prompted me to scan my membership card. At first, I was confused because I wasn’t sure how my deals from the app would transfer over. This didn’t match my mental model, which is what I expected to happen based on my past experiences. I thought the app would automatically apply the deals, but instead I had to enter my phone number. After a worker helped me figure that out, the process started to make more sense.


Once I began scanning my items, the experience felt smooth and familiar. The scanner made a clear beep after each item, which is an example of good feedback, meaning the system was clearly communicating that my action worked. It was easy to understand what to do because I’ve seen cashiers scan items many times before, so I already had an idea of how the process should work.

However, when I finished scanning and tried to move on to payment, the machine suddenly stopped me and displayed a message saying there were still items in my cart that needed to be scanned. This was confusing because I had already scanned everything I planned to buy. It turned out the system detected my backpack and helmet as unscanned items. This is an example of error prevention, where the system tries to stop users from making mistakes, but in this case it was too sensitive and created a problem instead.

The message itself wasn’t very clear, which made the situation more frustrating. Even though the system was trying to help, the lack of clear explanation made it harder to understand what was wrong. I had to wait for a worker to come over and confirm that those items were not part of my purchase. After pressing a few buttons, I was finally able to continue and complete the checkout.

Overall, parts of the experience worked well, especially the scanning process, which felt intuitive and easy to follow. However, the interruption caused by the system misidentifying items in my cart slowed everything down and made the self-checkout less convenient than expected.
